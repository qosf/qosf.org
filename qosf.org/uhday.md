## General Overview

You’re invited to participate in our local **UnitaryHACK Day**, an in-person event that is part of the global hackathon organized by the :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}.

With the support of mentors and a community of quantum enthusiasts, you will collaborate on open-source projects, contribute to real repositories, and gain hands-on experience in quantum computing.

---

## Why attend?

Many people interested in quantum computing face challenges such as:

- Limited access to hands-on, collaborative environments  
- Difficulty finding guidance when contributing to open-source projects  
- Lack of local communities to exchange ideas and learn together  

This event aims to address these challenges by bringing people together in a collaborative space where learning, building, and networking happen simultaneously.

## What is unitaryHACK?

**unitaryHACK** is a global open-source hackathon where participants contribute to quantum computing projects by submitting pull requests to participating repositories.

During the event period (June 3–17, 2026), contributors can:

- Work on real-world quantum software  
- Collaborate with developers and researchers  
- Earn bounties for accepted contributions  

---

## What is a HACKday?

HACKdays are local, in-person events held during the unitaryHACK period.

They provide an opportunity to:

- Collaborate with others in real time  
- Receive guidance from experienced mentors  
- Share ideas and explore new tools  

At the NYU HACKday, participants will experience:

- Opening remarks  
- A talk from an IBM speaker  
- Open hacking sessions with mentor support  

---

## Who should attend?

This event is open to:

- Students  
- Researchers  
- Developers  
- Quantum computing enthusiasts  

No matter your experience level, you are welcome. Whether you are making your first open-source contribution or already have experience, this event is designed to support your growth.
